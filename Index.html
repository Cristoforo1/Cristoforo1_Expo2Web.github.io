VPN, Deep Web and navigation safety


What is a VPN?

A virtual private network (VPN) encrypts your personal data and hides your real IP address on a public network.


It is one of the most common solutions to protect privacy and security on the Internet.


In addition to its privacy and security benefits, a VPN allows you to access more Internet content. With it, you can change your virtual location and avoid geo-blocked web pages.

When you connect to the Internet without a virtual private network, your Internet Service Provider (ISP) is the main intermediary between your device and the Internet. Your ISP assigns a unique IP address to your device and tracks all the websites you visit.


When using a VPN, your device's Internet connection still goes through your Internet provider. However, because it's encrypted and routed through a VPN server, your ISP will no longer see the websites you visit. It only knows that you are connected to a VPN and that encrypted traffic travels from your device to a server.

There are several types of VPNs that are used for different purposes. Let's look at four of the most common: personal, remote access, site-to-site, and mobile VPN.

The surface web or the open web

The open web or surface web is the “visible” surface layer.


From a statistical point of view, this set of websites and data constitutes less than 5% of the entire Internet.

Deep Web

The Deep Web lies beneath the surface and accounts for approximately 90% of all websites.


The term “deep web” refers to all web pages that search engines cannot identify. Deep web sites can hide behind passwords or other security walls, while others simply tell search engines not to “crawl” them.


While many media outlets use the “deep web” and the “dark web” interchangeably, much of the deep web as a whole is perfectly legal and safe.

Databases: Protected collections of both public and private files that are not connected to other areas of the web, only to be searched within the database itself.

Intranets: internal networks of companies, governments and educational facilities used to communicate and control private aspects within their organizations.
